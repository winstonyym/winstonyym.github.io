About Me

I am pursuing a PhD in Urban Analytics and 3D City Modelling, supervised by Prof. Filip Biljecki. My research interest is in open-source software, urban deep learning models, and volunteered geographic information. I am interested in the application of computational methods to inform the planning of sustainable and liveable cities.

Recent Work

Free and Open Urbanism: Software for Urban Planning Practice

Published in Computers, Environment and Urban Systems, Vol 96

A state of the art review of open source analytical software to support urban planners. We identified 124 software and analyse trends in the landscape. Our work is open source and available here.

Enhancing Semantic Understanding of Streetscapes through Networks

Under Review

We propose a novel, open workflow combining computer vision and network science to improve active mobility planning. We demonstrate our method through two case studies in Japan and Singapore.
